Shabu

Description

Shabu is a traditional Japanese hotpot dish where thinly sliced meat, often beef or pork, and fresh vegetables are briefly cooked in a pot of boiling broth.

Ingredients

  • Thinly sliced meat or pork
  • Fresh vegetables as follows:
    • Cabbage
    • Carrot
    • Spinach leaves
    • Green onion
  • Mushrooms as follows:
    • Shiitake mushrooms
    • Enoki mushrooms
  • Shrimp
  • Tofu
  • Udon noodles or cooked rice
  • Dashi broth
  • Ponzu sauce
  • Sesame sauce

Steps

  1. Prepare the broth
    • In a large pot, bring the dashi broth to a gentle simmer. Place it at the center of the table on a portable stove for communal cooking.
  2. Arrange Ingredients
    • Thinly sliced meat or pork
    • Cabbage, carrot, spinach leaves, and green onion (chopped as needed)
    • Shiitake and enoki mushrooms
    • Shrimp (cleaned and deveined)
    • Tofu (cut into cubes)
    • Udon noodles or cooked rice
  3. Set Up the Dipping Sauces
    • Place individual bowls of ponzu sauce and sesame sauce at each seat for dipping.
  4. Cook the Ingredients
    • Begin by adding a few vegetables, tofu, shrimp, and mushrooms to the simmering dashi broth. Let them cook for a few minutes.
    • Using chopsticks or tongs, swish a slice of meat or pork in the hot broth until it cooks.
  5. Eat as You Go
